Framing Thesis

Anduril's competitive edge is not just its technology — it is the speed at which it moves from concept to production. That speed is physically constrained by infrastructure: factories that don't exist yet, labs that need to be stood up in months not years, secure spaces that must work on day one. The Infrastructure Strategy & Operations role exists to eliminate that constraint. It is an operations and analytical problem: how do you build a decision-making and execution engine that keeps physical infrastructure from becoming the bottleneck in a product-driven defense company? That is the problem I want to work on — and the matrix below maps why I am positioned to do it.
